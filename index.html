Directions

DRIVE

Anacortes is going to be your destination before boarding the ferry. Once there, you'll purchase a round-trip ticket to Friday Harbor, which is the only ferry terminal on San Juan Island. After disembarking the ferry, Roche Harbor is a short, 20-minute drive through rural farmland and forests.

Map directions from your starting point to the Anacortes Ferry Terminal.

Map directions from the Friday Harbor ferry terminal to Roche Harbor's check-in area.

Be sure to check the ferry schedule to find out what options you have for the trip over to the island. Also, keep in mind that you don't want to miss the boat, so be sure to show up 60-90 minutes prior to the scheduled departure time to increase your odds of making the sailing.

Parking at the Anacortes Ferry Terminal

During the summer months the ferry wait on weekends can be up to two hours, so many people decide to drive to the Anacortes ferry terminal, park at the terminal, and then walk on to the ferry. Shuttles and taxis run throughout the day from Friday Harbor (disembarkation) to Roche Harbor.

SHUTTLE FROM SEA-TAC AIRPORT OR SEATTLE

If you choose to leave the car at home and still want to experience the ferry ride from Anacortes, two shuttle services make the run from Seattle to the ferry terminal.

Island Airporter operates Monday through Saturday, and has guaranteed boarding on the ferry that takes you directly to Roche Harbor. The non-stop service departs Sea-Tac at 12pm, and arrives in Roche Harbor at 4pm directly in front of the Hotel de Haro. $49.99 each way, or $56.54 each way including ferry fare.

Bellair Shuttle offers multiple runs every day of the week, with pick-up/drop-off in Sea-Tac or downtown Seattle to the Anacortes ferry terminal, or southbound for your return home. Rates are $50-65 each way, not including ferry fare.

Roche Harbor Accommodations

Roche Harbor offers a variety of accommodations. For reservations, please call (800) 451-8910 and let them know that you are with the Davis 2014 wedding block. All prices are per night.

Hotel de Haro - $99 to $135

These simple, historic hotel rooms in our Hotel de Haro feature all the charm of yester-year and close proximity for easy access to all resort amenities. All guestrooms offer a comfortable sleeping environment in small spaces with the convenience of a wash basin in the room. Many rooms feature furniture original to the hotel from its inception in 1886.

NOTE: Only 4 of the 20 hotel rooms have toilets or showers in the room. Access to four shared bathrooms are down the hallway - reminiscent of a traditional 19th-century layout. Only the four suites have private bathrooms.

View details »

Quarryman Hall - $355 to $445

Our newest addition to deluxe offerings, the Quarryman Hall rests majestically in the center of the village next to the Hotel de Haro. This boutique-inspired hotel features contemporary amenities, while instilling the ambience of the turn of the century.

View details »

McMillin Suites - $495

Four deluxe suites occupy a historic home perched on a bluff overlooking the harbor. Spacious decks have magnificent sunset views, perfect for ending the day on a romantic note. These accommodations are classically decorated and feature comfortable sitting areas, king beds, oversize bathrooms with heated floors and claw foot tubs. All suites are pet-friendly.

View details »

Historic Cottages - $299 to $350

These cottages are our most popular places to stay during the spring, summer and fall months. Each one has two bedrooms and a fully equipped kitchen, making them the perfect place for a family or two couples to relax in comfort. The five front-row cottages have full views of the harbor and sunsets, and feature a gas fireplace to warm the great room. If the weather's nice, all cottages have bbq, picnic table and firepit with wood - a perfect setting for creating memories on your visit to Roche Harbor.

View details »

3 & 4 Bedroom Village Homes - $675 to $900

Spacious and sophisticated, our village homes are designed with family comforts in mind. All homes feature an open great-room floorplan for the living room, dining area and kitchen, so you can relax and connect with loved ones. Gourmet kitchens are fully equipped with high-end appliances, a wide variety of culinary tools, and place settings to host memorable family meals. Each home has an outdoor space for barbequing and enjoying the territorial views, creating opportunities to enjoy the pastoral island way. Combined with the creature comforts of your own home, like wifi and satellite TV, you're sure to find our most deluxe accommodations are a welcome venue to host your island stay.
